Vector Analysis - Areas of interest for moving in Hamburg

  • Tools: ESRI ArcMap, ModelBuilder
  • Data: ESRI Deutschland Open Data

    • About the data

    The datasets correspond, in the majority, to Hamburg. They were downloaded from the ESRI Deutschland Open Data Portal. There is a data set corresponding to the municipalities of all Germany, used to set Hamburg in context. A dataset containing the city divisions in Hamburg was used to delineate the area of study. Additionally, there is a line data set used for the bike routes and two point data sets corresponding to the public transport stations and park locations.

    The Processing

    The processing was done using ModelBuilder. Moreover, Buffers were created to establish distances according to how much I was willing to move to get to the desired destination. 500 meters for parks, 1000 m for public transportation stations, and 1000 m for bike routes, since I can reach the latter by cycling. These Buffers were then intersected to discriminate interesting areas.

    The results show a group of zones in the central area of the city. However, there are a couple of areas of interest on the south side of the city. The main conclusion being that the best zone to look for a place to move in would be in the middle of the city. There was an additional analysis. There is a dataset available with all the addresses in the city, which contains 276,411 addresses. After having the zones of interest polygon, I applied an intersection with this addresses dataset, I obtained 9390 interesting addresses. To me, this is a good example of how to filter data by using spatial analysis.
